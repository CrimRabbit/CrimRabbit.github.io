A statue
Wed 03 May 2017 Writing blurbs

“Hey, I need you to come over tomorrow afternoon yeah? It’s about this statue I made. Need you to appraise it for sale.” Just like that, he hung up the phone. As if expecting that an art appraiser could shake her boots on the table, free to take last minute calls for appraising. I had a schedule, damnit! Admittedly, Michal’s works are pretty sought after; keeping the sculptor of the David statue hidden was tiring, but well worth the cost. Michal agreeing to exclusively allow me to appraise and basically be the one to show his works to the world was the biggest boon to my career since entering art school. Luckily for me, I had the “studio mate” friendship card to play. Still, his working habits were.. dubious as best.

Entering the dimly lit studio, I called out, hoping to need not wander aimlessly around this mess. “Hey Michal, I’m here. Where are you?” “Just a minute! Looking for the light switch now. God only knows how a woman like you can’t work in the dark.”

“It’s stranger to be perfectly fine in the dark!” I wanted to retort. Yet the visage of a recently finished sculpture stood before me. Even in the dark, the signs of utmost care in creating was apparent. Every minute detail was meticulously looked over, and summarily perfected. Her eyes, lifelike as if watching me observe it. Her body, graceful as a scupture of a goddess. Her skin, smooth and clear like porcelain. From each of her strands of hair to each individual nail, She looked like someone out of a work of art. It was clear that Michal put alot of work into this.

The lights flicked on then, and its mouth moved. “Hello! I see we haven’t met! Come in, come in, introduce yourself!”

The statue talked.

“Hehe, yes I’m crafted with the human female as a base. Given how happy Michal was when he called you, I’m pretty confident in my looks too! I even took my gender into consideration and tried putting on some female clothing!”

For someone, no, something who looked like she walked out of a work of art, I could do nothing but stare dumbfoundedly. The haphazard way her clothes were put on aside, she possessed beauty beyond perfection. And that same person was originally not even human!

“Hmm? So is there some part of me that’s off? I believe Michal captured the proper anatomy of people in the world… Or is this strip of cloth here not really a headband but a skirt?”

And that same statue had some warped sense of the world right now, given her creator. How am I even going to appraise her?

- Joe Ng
