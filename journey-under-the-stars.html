Journey under the stars
Sun 26 March 2017 Writing short

We both looked up at the same sky that night. That star-filled sky, milky way streaking across, painted a visage of hope, of a million hopes and dreams. You told me, “Let’s make it come true! You always wanted to see the world right, Regan? Now I’m going to drag you everywhere!” I could only smile in shock back then. The world was vast, fraught with dangers unknown! Yet that brilliant smile on your moonlit face challenged whatever dangers that sat within my heart. And so we went forth, leaving the Town of Beginnings to see for our young selves what the world had to offer.

The roads indeed were wrought with dangers. Even if our travels were off the back of a merchant’s caravan, we were attacked by wolves, heavy rains and even black-hearted adventurers. Every night, either of us would stay awake, watching over the other as we rested. Clonk, clonk, clonk, the wheels would go, and the horse would occasionally snort in exertion. Some days we saw mountain ranges as far as the eye could see. Other days would be filled with fog, where even the moonlight would be blocked from out view. Yet one thing never changed. You remained smiling, full of good cheer regardless of the adversity. “A smile makes another smile! Even if the going is tough, if we fall into despair we will lose! So smile!” you would say.

The world was wonderous indeed, we saw vast plains of wheat, lakes so clear we could see the bottom, nights so clear we counted the stars and their consellations. Together, just as you said. Everyday, there would be a wonder that’d take my breath away. Some days, we would go hungry from the long road, and share a measly loaf of bread. Other days, we would laugh around a campfire as we revelled with other adventurers, sharing stories of the road. Do you remember? Olivia sung without reservation that star-filled night, where the caravan rolled without stop due to tight deadlines.

I prayed that night, our journey would never end. Even if we ran out of energy, even if we saw all of the world, I wanted these days to go on forever. These days of wonder. You nodded then, leaning onto my shoulder. “If you ever feel lost, look to the stars. We of the road can both find the North star and find our hearts in the stars.” Indeed. As I glanced at the face resting on my shoulder, gazing at the night sky, I found that to be true. The stars reflected in your eyes that night, reflected all of my heart.

- Joe Ng
